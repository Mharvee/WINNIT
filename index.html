01 · Executive Summary

WinIt isn't competing with other raffle apps. It's competing with a Lagos user's instinct to assume every prize platform is fake.

That instinct is rational it's been earned by years of advance-fee scams, fake giveaway pages, and "you've won" messages that ask for money before they pay any out. This document treats that instinct as the actual product problem, and redesigns the draw experience the single moment where trust is made or lost around a simple test applied to every screen: does this make a skeptical user trust WinIt more, or less?

What follows is not a visual refresh. It's a working prototype of nine redesigned touchpoints, a named framework you can apply to future features, one recommendation we've deliberately built differently than requested with the business reasoning laid out in full and a phased roadmap your team can execute against directly.

02 · The Real Competitor

Understanding why Lagos users hesitate and what actually changes their mind

Nigeria has one of the world's most scam-literate consumer bases, not because Nigerians are naive, but because they've had to become experts at spotting fraud. Advance-fee schemes, fake "clearance fee" lottery messages, and impersonation scams via personal WhatsApp numbers are common enough that most adults in Lagos can describe the pattern from memory. A digital raffle platform inherits all of that suspicion by default before a single screen loads.

At the same time, this same audience now has a much higher bar for what "trustworthy digital product" looks like, set by the fintech apps they use daily Paystack, Flutterwave, Kuda, PiggyVest. Those products taught users what real transparency feels like: visible numbers, plain-language explanations, support that actually responds. Any platform that falls short of that bar, even unintentionally, reads as suspicious by comparison.

What erodes trust vs. what builds it

✕ Erodes trust
  • Countdown timers and "hurry" copy with no real deadline behind them
  • Vague or hidden odds no visible ticket count or pool size
  • Winner announcements with no way to verify they're real
  • Generic support inboxes that never reply
  • Visual style that resembles low-quality betting sites
✓ Builds trust
  • Odds shown as a plain number, updated live, no marketing spin
  • A random result that can be mathematically checked after the fact
  • Explicit, in-app statements about what the platform will never ask for
  • Licensing and registration numbers published, not buried
  • Lagos-based support a person can actually reach
03 · Our Framework

The Glass Box Standard

Physical raffle draws earned trust for one structural reason: the ball machine was made of glass. You could see there was no trick, without anyone having to tell you so. A digital draw has no such default transparency it has to be engineered in, deliberately, screen by screen. That's the standard we designed every recommendation in this document against.

👁️
Pillar 01

See It

Odds, pool size, and personal spend are shown as plain numbers, live, with no marketing language between the user and the fact. If it can be shown honestly, it's shown.

🔍
Pillar 02

Check It

The result is backed by a published hash, a revealed seed, and a public license number three things a skeptical user or a journalist could independently verify, not just a badge that says "trusted."

🗣️
Pillar 03

Know It

Designed for Lagos specifically: Naira and WAT throughout, an explicit anti-scam statement addressing the exact fraud pattern users already fear, and support that's local, not offshore.

✋
Pillar 04

Respect It

No fake countdowns, no invented scarcity, no social-proof numbers that aren't real. Restraint is itself a trust signal the absence of manipulation is something users notice, even unconsciously.

05 · Strategic Spotlight

Why ticket sales stay closed during a live draw

One recommendation from the working session was to explore keeping ticket purchases open through certain stages of the live draw or countdown, to create urgency and lift sales. We implemented this differently, and want to walk through the reasoning rather than just note the change.

⚠️Recommendation: keep the pool locked once a draw is visibly computing
The ask
Allow ticket purchases during the live draw or late countdown to increase urgency and ticket volume.
The risk
This document's opening premise is that WinIt's real competitor is skepticism. Selling tickets for a draw while that same draw is visibly computing its result reopens the exact question every other screen in this redesign was built to close: "was the winner already known when I bought?" One inconsistency like this generalizes a user who notices it stops trusting the seal, the licensing block, and the verification call too.
Our recommendation
Keep the existing rule: sales close 20 seconds before the draw starts and stay closed until the next draw opens. This is the one mechanic in this document we'd advise against relaxing, even under commercial pressure.
What you get instead
The Live and Result screens (see section 04) now carry a cross-sell carousel "tickets for this draw are locked, but these are open now" pointing at other active campaigns. WinIt keeps selling through the entire live moment. It simply never sells the draw that's mid-reveal. Same commercial mechanism, without the trust cost.

07 · Before You Ship

Four things this design assumes will be true

None of these are design problems they're operational commitments the interface makes on WinIt's behalf. Each one needs an owner before launch.

Real license and CAC numbers
The licensing block is a placeholder. A fabricated registration number is worse for trust than none at all replace it only with verifiable details.
Real live engagement numbers
Viewer counts and ticket totals must reflect actual activity. An inflated "watching now" counter is the same category of dark pattern as a fake countdown.
A resourced verification-call process
The Verify screen promises a real call to every winner within an hour. Someone on the team needs to own that operationally before it's promised in-app.
A support line that actually answers
The "Lagos-based support" badge is a claim, not a decoration it should only ship once WhatsApp/phone support is genuinely staffed.
